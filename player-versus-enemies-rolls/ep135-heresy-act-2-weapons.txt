//notes:Inspired by @PodvsEnemies episode 135 from 25 March 2024 (https://open.spotify.com/episode/5JwIN3avdyW27aRjv4Zb2D?si=21b247e788174887) with @CourtProjects, @Saint_Kabr and @ImpetusAlways (PvE PvE-MinorSpec PvE-God PvE-EndGame M+KB Controller): First-choice PvE pick for clearing groups of Minor adds in both general at-level or better or end-game underleveled content, particularly with Void builds. Unfortunately, Anamnesis is in the Lightweight subfamily of bows, so although you do get the frame intrinsic +20 Mobility and ×1.0625 movement speed bonus, that's at the cost of accuracy and damage. At base, the stats are in-band - 32 Accuracy, 50 Stability is ×1.1844 flinch resist at 100 Resilience and a perfect draw window of 0.65s, 64 Handling is 0.55s ready, 0.41s ADS and 0.42s stow speed, 60 Reload Speed is a 0.68s reload, and Recoil Direction is 40. For void builds, there are the trait perk combinations of Repulsor Break, for a void overshield on a void-debuffed kill in the first column, together with either Destabilizing Rounds, to spread AoE volatility and proc volatile rounds for 4.5s on kills, or Demoralize to spread AoE weaken for an effective ×1.18 damage boost against enemies. If you're interested, "Anamnesis" is the act of recalling something to mind. Willing Vessel is a useful, if unspectacular origin trait to boost gunfeel stats in the background as you hit and kill targets, but if you're in a fireteam, and manually reloading, Runneth Over will overfill your magazine by an additional 10% (rounded up) for each Guardian you're within 15m of. Recommended MW: Draw Time, with a Quick Access Sling mod or, when masterworked, Synergy to create Void Breaches or, when the artifact mod's around, Stunloader. //notes:Inspired by @PodvsEnemies episode 135 from 25 March 2024 (https://open.spotify.com/episode/5JwIN3avdyW27aRjv4Zb2D?si=21b247e788174887) with @CourtProjects, @Saint_Kabr and @ImpetusAlways (PvE PvE-MinorSpec PvE-EndGame M+KB Controller): First-choice PvE pick for use against Minor adds in both general at-level or better, or end-game underleveled content, particularly with melee builds. Refusal of the Call is the first adaptive frame strand glaive, which is the subfamily with the best shield duration, if that's your play. At base, the stats are okay - 64 Range equates to 18.49m and a velocity of 111.28m/s, 44 Shield Duration is 11.49s, 60 Handling is 0.30s ready and 0.35s stow speed, 43 Reload Speed is a 2.75s reload, and magazine size is 6 (max 8). With 0/1/2/3 reserves mods, total ammo is 30/36/40/42 (max 42). There are a couple of Haft perks that can help boost shield duration, but they have a mix of bonuses and maluses, so go with that suits your playstyle best. For the magazine perk, either look for something that helps with mag size or reload. There are a few decent trait perks in the first column to help with reload or to avoid reloading altogether - Auto-Loading Holster, Envious Assassin, Impulse Amplifier and Discord. In the second column, pair that together with Close to Melee to boost the glaive melee damage by ×1.3 for 10s on a projectile hit  (refreshable for 5s on melee or projectile hits), or Disruption Break which provides a ×1.5 boost to glaive melee damage for 5.5s if you break an enemy's shield with a projectile. Willing Vessel is a useful, if unspectacular origin trait to boost gunfeel stats in the background as you hit and kill targets, but if you're in a fireteam, and manually reloading, Runneth Over will overfill your magazine by an additional 10% (rounded up) for each Guardian you're within 15m of. Recommended MW: Handling or Reload Speed, with a Backup Mag mod or, when masterworked, Synergy to create Strand Tangles or, when the artifact mod's around, Stunloader. Also, don't forget to check whether there's a useful Seasonal Fragile mod for the activity you're running.|tags:PvE PvE-MinorSpec PvE-EndGame M+KB Controller
