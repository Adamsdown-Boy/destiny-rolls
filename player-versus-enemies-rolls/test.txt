//notes:Inspired by @PodvsEnemies episode 153 from 9 September 2025 (https://open.spotify.com/episode/6iEksMDGzT5euNuMAEqnVm?si=a9bf49987cdb441a) with @CourtProjects and @Saint_Kabr (PvE PvE-Utility PvE-God PvE-EndGame M+KB Controller): First-choice PvE utility roll for movement tech in general at-level or better content. Synanceia is a wave frame, so the heavy wave attack costs 4 ammo with an extra 1 for the optional targeted follow-up. At base, by comparison with its peers, the stats look good - Impact and Charge Rate are both highest in the subfamily, and they're improved further at Tier 4/5 when blade and guard perks are enhanced - see https://d2foundry.gg/w/1823308496 for how the weapon stats impact in-game performance. Base total ammo is (check in game*). Normally we'd use the blade perk to maximise damage but, as this is an Eager Edge roll, the more ammo you have, the more sword lunges. And, for this particular roll, the guard perk doesn't really matter. The first trait perk column doesn't have anything that directly helps with Eager Edge but, if you're using this against adds it might as well do something useful. Sharp Harvest returns 5% of special ammo reserves for 3 hits within ~1.5s (~2.0s when enhanced), Relentless Strikes returns 1 ammo if you get 3 hits within ~2s of each other (~2.5s when enhanced) or Attrition Orbs spawns a power orb after 10 hits within 5s of each other - hits with the wave attack count towards the counter. Really, all of this is just the delivery system for a Edge of Fate featured Eager Edge sword that provides a ×1.8 increased grounded lunge and ×1.64 airborne lunge (×2 and ×1.8 when enhanced) for 3s after readying. The Tenacity Origin trait stacks up to ×20 on kills, providing +2.5 Handling and Reload Speed and and extra 0.5% damage per stack (max +50 Handling and Reload, and ×1.1 damage boost) until you die. There's also Häkke Breach Armaments - if you want to attack a tank, turret or Shrieker with a sword then good luck with that! If you're interested, Synanceia is the scientific genus for stonefish, which are venomous, marine ray-finned fish found in tropical Indo-Pacific waters and the Red Sea. These fish are known for their camouflage, resembling rocks, and possess potent neurotoxins in their dorsal spines that can cause severe pain, paralysis, and even death in humans.
